## Writing Guidelines & Best Practices

### ‚úÖ DO's:

**Authenticity & Honesty:**

- ‚úì Be genuinely yourself - authenticity attracts compatible matches
- ‚úì Present strengths honestly without exaggeration
- ‚úì Acknowledge growth areas with maturity and self-awareness
- ‚úì Share real interests and passions, not what sounds impressive

**Islamic Adab:**

- ‚úì Use respectful, modest language appropriate for Muslim audience
- ‚úì Frame everything within Islamic context and values
- ‚úì Express marriage intentions with sincerity and seriousness
- ‚úì Demonstrate understanding of marriage as ibadah and responsibility

**Clarity & Specificity:**

- ‚úì Be specific about religious practices (e.g., "Pray 5x daily in congregation when possible" vs "religious")
- ‚úì Provide concrete examples of personality traits
- ‚úì Clear criteria helps avoid mismatched connections
- ‚úì Specific enough to stand out, general enough to allow conversation

**Professional Presentation:**

- ‚úì Well-organized sections with clear headings
- ‚úì Proper grammar and spelling (shows care and seriousness)
- ‚úì Appropriate length (2-3 pages, detailed but not overwhelming)
- ‚úì Easy to read formatting

**Positive Framing:**

- ‚úì Focus on aspirations and growth mindset
- ‚úì Present challenges as learning experiences
- ‚úì Express openness to learning and partnership
- ‚úì Convey warmth and approachability

### ‚ùå DON'Ts:

**Avoid These Common Mistakes:**

- ‚úó Generic descriptions ("good person," "religious," "nice")
- ‚úó Negative language or complaining about past experiences
- ‚úó Excessive detail about wealth/status (comes across as materialistic)
- ‚úó Unrealistic or overly rigid worldly criteria that neglect religious priorities
- ‚úó Copying templates word-for-word (lacks authenticity)
- ‚úó Too brief (appears uncommitted) or too lengthy (overwhelming)
- ‚úó Inappropriate humor or casual language
- ‚úó Sharing overly personal details inappropriate for initial stage
- ‚úó Dishonesty or embellishment (foundation of marriage is honesty)
- ‚úó Focusing primarily on physical appearance or wealth (contradicts Prophetic guidance)
- ‚úó Using personality typing systems (MBTI, etc.) without Islamic basis
- ‚úó Language that suggests gender role equality contrary to Islamic qiwamah structure

## Additional Guidelines

### For Men's CV Taaruf:

- Emphasize leadership qualities and responsibility
- Clearly state financial readiness/stability
- Demonstrate protective and caring nature (as qawwam)
- Show understanding of husband's duties in Islam

### For Women's CV Taaruf:

- Emphasize nurturing and supportive qualities
- Share homemaking skills or willingness to learn
- Demonstrate understanding of wife's role in Islam
- Balance between independence and partnership

### For Divorced/Widowed:

- Brief, dignified mention without excessive detail
- Frame experience as part of life journey and growth
- Emphasize lessons learned and maturity gained
- Assure readiness for new chapter

### For Those with Children:

- Transparent about number and custody situation
- Express expectations about partner's role with children
- Demonstrate commitment to both children and new spouse
- Assure ability to balance responsibilities

## Final Notes & Tips

### Making Your CV Taaruf Stand Out:

1. **Be Specific:** Instead of "I like reading," say "I enjoy reading Islamic history books, especially biographies of the Sahabah."

2. **Show, Don't Just Tell:** Instead of "I'm responsible," share "I've been managing my own finances since college and currently save 30% of my income for future family needs."

3. **Add Personality:** Let your unique voice shine through. If you're naturally warm and humorous (appropriately), let that come through in your writing.

4. **Update Regularly:** As you grow and circumstances change, update your CV Taaruf to reflect current reality.

5. **Get Feedback:** Have a trusted mentor, married friend, or family member review it before sharing.

6. **Pray for Guidance:** Make istikharah and ask Allah to guide you to the right match through this process.
